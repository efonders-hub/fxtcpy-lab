Why the hook change didn't work

You Have Already Changed the Hook.
Here Is Why That Did Not Work.

A hook change treats what the viewer sees first. It does not touch what happens in the 60 to 120 seconds that follow. That window is where the real failure lives.

Inside that window, a viewer hits a structural belief checkpoint. If the copy does not clear it, they stop watching. The opening did not earn the next step in their internal logic.

A new hook gets more people past the first five seconds. It does not change what happens at the checkpoint. The gap is still there. The number comes back down.

The structural read names which checkpoint is failing and why. That gives the copywriter a brief. A brief produces a fix that holds, because it addresses the actual cause.

The failure sits 60 to 120 seconds in, at a structural belief checkpoint the hook never reaches. A new hook buys a few more seconds. It does not fix the checkpoint.
