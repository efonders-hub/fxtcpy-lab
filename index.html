Before We Go Any Further

Stop Doing These Five Things

Before I show you what actually fixes a bleeding VSL, you need to stop doing the five things that are making it worse.

  • 1

    Stop Testing New Hooks

    If your lead structure is broken, a new hook just camouflages the leak for a week or two before the numbers crash again. Hook testing assumes your lead is sound. When the lead has structural problems, new hooks create temporary spikes followed by deeper drops.

    Stop burning budget on hook tests. The problem is not the first three seconds. The next 87 are where you lose them.

  • 2

    Stop Hiring New Copywriters

    If the original lead has a structural belief leak, better words will not fix it. You get beautiful copy that bleeds just as badly as the original. Sometimes worse, because the new writer does not understand why the old version worked in the first place.

    You do not need another copywriter. You need a diagnostician who can find structural problems.

  • 3

    Stop Blaming Creative Fatigue

    Real creative fatigue takes months of heavy impression frequency to develop. Most funnels that burn out in weeks were never structurally sound. They worked because of launch momentum or a friendly algorithm window. The lead was never built to last.

    A VSL that drops from strong to weak in weeks is not suffering fatigue. Traffic volume exposed a structural flaw.

  • 4

    Stop Adding More Proof

    Proof added to a structurally broken lead feels desperate. The viewer has already decided something does not add up. Piling on testimonials makes the whole thing feel more suspicious, not less.

    Strong proof in a weak structure is like expensive paint on a house with a cracked foundation. It does not fool anyone, and it wastes your money.

  • 5

    Stop Overthinking Your Avatar

    Buyer belief breaks down the same way across demographics. A structural leak in your lead bleeds conversions whether your viewer is 25 or 55, whether they make $50K or $500K.

    The person matters less than the moment. That moment happens in the first 90 seconds, regardless of who is watching.

"But my VSL is in a completely different niche than your examples..."

The niche does not matter. Buyer belief works the same way across all markets. I have diagnosed VSLs in markets ranging from weight loss to cryptocurrency to real estate. The seven leak points are universal because human psychology is universal.

Your niche makes the diagnostic easier, not harder, because I can spot patterns specific to your market that general copywriters miss.

"What if the problem is not in the first 90 seconds? What if it is later in the video?"

When conversions drop across the board, the problem is almost always in the lead structure. If viewers were making it to minute five before dropping off, you would see partial conversions — people clicking to the sales page but not buying.

When conversions crater entirely, viewers are leaving before they ever get emotionally invested. That happens in the first 90 seconds.

"My VSL already has strong credibility and social proof. How could there be a structural leak?"

This is one of the most common patterns I see. You have great proof, but it is in the wrong place or framed the wrong way. Strong credibility in a broken structure often feels more suspicious, not less.

Sometimes the leak is not missing proof. The proof arrives too late. Or it interrupts the belief-building sequence. The 7-Leak Framework finds those timing and positioning issues that sabotage even the strongest credentials.

"What if I am not spending enough on traffic to get meaningful data?"

If you are spending real money daily and seeing consistent patterns over at least a week, you have enough data. The framework looks for structural issues. Statistical variation is not what it measures.
