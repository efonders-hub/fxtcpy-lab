StoryBrand Website Examples

Browse these galleries and specific sites before briefing David on a new page build. Pick one site or call out elements from several. Then come back with a reference point.

What to look for as you browse

  • Does the above-the-fold headline name the reader's problem before it names anything else?
  • Does the color palette feel commanding and professional, or warm and approachable? (The Fader needs commanding.)
  • Are the sections visually distinct, or does the page bleed together?
  • Does the CTA button stand out or blend into the design?
  • Does the guide positioning feel credible without being boastful?
  • When something stops you, even if you can't explain why - note the URL. That's your reference.

How to use this page

Start with the galleries

Scroll until something stops you. You do not need to explain why it stopped you. The URL is enough. Bring that back.

You can mix and match

"The layout from site A, the color feel from site B, the headline treatment from site C" is a complete brief. Pick elements, not a single template.

What to bring back to David

One or two URLs. What you liked. What you did not. The rest is David's call.

Design feel vs. copy structure

Note whether you are reacting to the visual design or the message structure. Both are useful. They are separate inputs to the build.
